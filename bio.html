Zaki studied film and media production at UCSD and has been working in the entertainment industry ever since. She has worked as an actress, producer, DP and director and has made commercials, corporate videos, web series, TV series (non-scripted) and feature films.

Zaki's creativity, knack for story, and imaginative approach has kept her steadly employed for years. She has directed reenactments for over 50 episodes of true crime including the Lifetime network docuseries Phrogging: Hider in My House as well as the Oxygen network docu-shows A Wedding and a Murder. She is also a prolific commercial director, being part of a team that won two Telly Awards for broadcast ads.

Most recently she worked on 16 hours of content for the micro-drama app GammaTime: a premium vertical-format streaming service backed by Bill Block, Kris Jenner, and Kim Kardashian. She also completed shooting the feature film Anatomy (drama) which will enter its festival run in 2027, and a short film Abides (thriller).

Zaki was born in Los Angeles and grew up in Israel and lived in France, and as an Officer in the Israeli Defense Force she learned invaluable leadership skills that she uses in all her productions.
